    MR with cis-pQTLs

    I applied Mendelian Randomization to the European-ancestry subset of the IBD GWAS of Liu et al.1, using cis-pQTLs from the UK Biobank Pharma Proteomics Project2 as instruments.

    Since each protein in the UKBB PPP has at most one cis-pQTL, I used the Wald Ratio to estimate the causal effects of the proteins on IBD.

    I used the R package TwoSampleMR.

    Results

    The plot below summarizes the effect sizes and standard errors of the Bonferroni-significant proteins found by MR analysis with cis-pQTLs. Effect sizes are expressed in IBD liability units per inverse-normal rank-transformed protein abundance unit.

    Mostly, these results make sense. For instance:

    • IL-12 is a cytokine linked to autoimmunity. It makes sense that increased levels of both the heterodimeric Il-12 (IL12A_IL12B) and the B sub-unit (IL12B) increase risk of IBD.
    • IL-10 is a powerful anti-inflammatory cytokine. It makes sense that increased levels of IL-10 reduce risk of IBD.

    However, the meaning of some of these results are less clear. For instance

    • CEP43 has been found in mouse models to be important to gut epithelial renewal3. But if this is the case, why would higher levels of CEP43 increase rather than reduce liability to inflammatory bowel disease? One possibility is that higher plasma levels of CEP43 may reciprocally correspond to lower gut levels, but this is speculation. Overall, the mechanism of action for this protein is unclear.
    • IL1R2 is an anti-inflammatory decoy receptor, so it is unclear why higher levels would be associated with a greater IBD liability.
