# UK Biobank

The UK Biobank[@sudlow2015uk] is a British prospective health study with with more than 500,000 participants. The UK Biobank was established with the aim of facilitating research on the causes of diseases of middle and old age.  As such, it recruited participants aged 40-69 years.  Running such a large study required the use of industrial-scale sample-processing and streamlined management.


## Phenotype

A key advantage of the UK biobank is its extensive phenotypic data.  This includes:

- Linked medical records from the UK's National Health Service.
- Responses to surveys, both at the initial enrollment visit and on follow-up.
- Test results, such as cardiac MRI[@pirruccello2022genetic] and exercise testing[@verweij2018genetic].
- [Results of the OLink Proteomics assay](UKBB_PPP.md)[@sun2023plasma].
