# UK Biobank

The UK Biobank[@sudlow2015uk] is a British prospective health study with more than 500,000 participants.

[//]: # (The UK Biobank was established with the aim of facilitating research on the causes of diseases of middle and old age.  As such, it recruited participants aged 40-69 years.  Running such a large study required the use of industrial-scale sample-processing and streamlined management.)


## Phenotype

A key advantage of the UK biobank is its extensive phenotypic data.  This includes:

- Linked medical records from the UK's National Health Service.
- Responses to surveys, both at the initial enrollment visit and on follow-up.
- Test results, such as cardiac MRI[@pirruccello2022genetic] and exercise testing[@verweij2018genetic].
- [Proteomics assay data](UKBB_PPP.md)[@sun2023plasma].


## Genotype

The genetics of UK Biobank participants has been extensively analyzed. This includes

- Initial chip-based genotyping of all participants[@bycroft2018uk].
- Subsequent exome sequencing of all participants[@wang2021rare].
- Subsequent short-read whole-genome sequencing of all participants[@uk2025whole].

There is [an ongoing project](https://community.ukbiobank.ac.uk/hc/en-gb/articles/34007463768221-Upcoming-data#:~:text=First%20release%20of%20Olink%20proteomics,read%20whole%20genome%20sequencing%E2%80%8B) to apply long-read whole genome sequencing to a subset of UK Biobank participants.
