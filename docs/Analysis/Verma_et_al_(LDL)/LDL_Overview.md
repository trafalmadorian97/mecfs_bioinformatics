# LDL

Cholesterol is transported in the blood via lipid-protein particles called low-density lipoproteins (LDL) (See Alberts et al. pg 791[@alberts2022molecular]).

It is now widely accepted that high levels of LDL in the blood cause atherosclerosis, which in turn increases the risk of a cardiovascular event.  The connection between LDL and cardiovascular risk was rancorously disputed over the course of the 20th century.  The story of this dispute is a classic case study in the history of science. It reveals the dynamics by which the accumulation of multiple independent lines of evidence can overthrow an established paradigm.
