# Chr20 Locus

In an attempt to narrow down the DecodeME[@genetics2025initial] GWAS-1 signal, I [fine-mapped](../../../Bioinformatics_Concepts/Fine_Mapping.md) the GWAS-1 hit on chromosome 20 using SUSIE[@wang2020simple].


As a linkage disequilibrium reference, I used a UK Biobank LD matrix hosted on [AWS Open Data](https://registry.opendata.aws/ukbb-ld/).  Because this LD reference uses GRCh37 coordinates, I used liftover to translate the DecodeME GWAS-1 summary statistics to GRCh37.

## Results with $L=10$

## Results with $L=10$ and strict threshold

The original paper on fine-mapping with SUSIE using summary statistics by Zou et al.[zou2022fine] notes that even slight mismatches between the population used to generate the GWAS summary statistics and the population from which the LD matrix is derived can cause SUSIE to become unstable or produce false positives.  Zou et al. suggest that filtering variants with a high likelihood ratio in a likelihood ratio test while also having high z score can mitigate this issue, since these variants are most impacted the mismatch.  By default, [consistent with the SUSIE documentation](https://stephenslab.github.io/susieR/reference/kriging_rss.html), my code filters variants that have a likelihood ratio of 2 or greater and a z score of 2 or greater.  To get sense of the sensitivity of SUSIE results to this filtering, I reran SUSIE with a z score threshold of 1. The results are below.
