# MAGMA HBA Analysis
I applied [MAGMA](../../Bioinformatics_Concepts/MAGMA_Overview.md) to the lupus GWAS of Bentham et al.[@bentham2015genetic]  using scRNAseq data from the [Human Brain Atlas](../../Data_Sources/HBA_scRNAseq.md)[@siletti2023transcriptomic] (HBA) as a reference.

The two independent clusters are a B-cell type and a microglia cell type.  

- Lupus is well known to be an immunoglobulin-driven disease. Since B-cells produce immunoglobulin, it is highly reasonable that the most strongly associated MAGMA cell type should be a group of B-cells.  
- There has also been some research suggesting a role of microglial activation in lupus.  Thus it is possible that the microglial signal indicates the real importance of microglia to lupus.  On the other hand, it is possible that the signal is a artifact of the similarity between microglial gene expression programs and the gene expression programs of other immune cells that truly drive lupus.
